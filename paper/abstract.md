## Abstract

We present the first weight-only 2-bit quantization of a Mixture-of-Experts language model, achieved entirely on a single 12 GB consumer laptop GPU. Our method is a minimal modification to QTIP's BlockLDLQ trellis quantization pipeline: we substitute routing-conditioned per-expert Hessians — collected from only the tokens the router dispatches to each expert — for the shared per-layer Hessian used in dense-LLM quantization. No algorithmic changes to the trellis quantizer, LDL decomposition, or QTIP HYB codebook are required. On OLMoE-1B-7B-0125, we achieve WikiText-2 perplexity 9.09 and C4 perplexity 14.16 at 2 bits per weight, against fp16 baselines of 6.65 and 12.24 (1.367x and 1.157x ratios respectively), with 71.15% zero-shot HellaSwag, 77.97% PIQA, and 44.28% ARC-Challenge accuracy. These numbers are competitive with the strongest published 3-bit MoE quantization results despite operating at one bit lower per weight. Ablation experiments show that per-expert Hessian calibration consistently beats both unweighted and token-weighted per-layer Hessian baselines by 0.09-0.28 PPL, that LUT fine-tuning of the codebook provides no additional benefit (3.0% MSE reduction on experts, 0.0% on attention), and that post-RHT MoE expert weights are numerically Gaussian (mean excess kurtosis 3.004 across 3072 matrices, std 0.01). The entire pipeline — Hessian collection, BlockLDLQ quantization, and full benchmark evaluation — runs end-to-end on a single NVIDIA RTX 4080 Laptop GPU (12 GB VRAM) in under 12 hours. Native 2-bit inference kernels for MoE are the natural next step and are deferred to future work.

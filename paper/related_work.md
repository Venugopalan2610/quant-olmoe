MoE Quantization. Recent work has begun adapting post-training quantization methods to mixture-of-experts architectures, motivated by the observation that the bulk of MoE parameter mass resides in expert FFN weights. MoEQuant [Hu et al., 2025] introduces two complementary techniques on top of GPTQ and AWQ: Expert-Balanced Self-Sampling (EBSS) for constructing calibration sets with uniform expert utilization, and Affinity-Guided Quantization (AGQ), which reweights a single per-layer Hessian by token-expert gating coefficients to incorporate routing information into the weight update. MoPEQ [X et al., 2025] proposes per-expert Hessian trace statistics for mixed-precision bit allocation, but uses the trace as a sensitivity scalar rather than as a calibration matrix. EAQuant [Y et al., 2025] applies expert-aware smoothing aggregation to AWQ. VEQ [Z et al., 2026] extends gating-aware reweighting to vision-language MoEs.
All of these methods build on uniform-grid quantizers and operate at 3-4 bits per weight, where the quantization noise floor is small enough that gating-aware reweighting of a shared Hessian provides meaningful improvement. Our work differs in three respects: (1) we collect fully independent per-expert Hessians from routing-conditioned forward hooks, eliminating the gate-coefficient reweighting approximation; (2) we apply trellis-coded quantization rather than uniform quantization, enabling sub-3-bit operating points; and (3) we evaluate at 2 bits per weight, a regime MoEQuant and its successors do not enter.
